How do work characteristics shape employee well-being and performance?

I am a Lecturer in Organizational Psychology at the University of Leeds. My research focuses on organizational behavior, including leadership, stress management, creativity, newcomer socialization, and workplace well-being.

I conduct research on how employees appraise workplace demands as either challenges or hindrances. This work demonstrates that appraisals can change over time and significantly impact employee wellbeing and performance. My research bridges theoretical insights with practical applications, helping organizations create healthier and more productive work environments.

I hold a Ph.D. in Organizational Psychology from Utrecht University, an M.A. in Applied Psychology from Renmin University of China, and a B.A. in Business Administration from North China Institute of Science and Technology.

Advanced Skills: Mplus, R, SPSS, conducting advanced Meta-analysis (e.g., multilevel meta-analysis, meta mediation analysis), SEM, Multi-level or longitudinal data analysis (e.g., latent transition analysis, cross-lagged panel analysis).
